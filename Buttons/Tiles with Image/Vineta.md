Vineta is a legendary trading city said to have once stood on the southern Baltic coast and to have been swallowed by the sea as punishment for its wealth and pride. On rare nights locals claim to hear bells or glimpse rooftops beneath the waves.

The legend likely grew from memories of prosperous medieval ports (Wolin/Jumne, Jomsborg) and from real coastal losses caused by storms and subsidence. Different coastal communities attached the tale to their own bays and sandbanks.

As a motif, Vineta symbolizes lost prosperity and a warning against hubris. It appears in chronicles, songs, and modern fiction and remains a popular setting for regional festivals and storytelling.
